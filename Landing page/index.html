Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system, with a diameter of about 4,880 kilometers (3,032 miles). It's only slightly larger than Earth's Moon.

Venus, the second planet from the Sun, is often considered Earth's "sister planet". With a diameter of approximately 12,104 kilometers (7,521 miles),it the second-largest terrestrial planet in our solar system.

Earth, the third planet from the Sun, stands out as the only known celestial body to support life. With a diameter of approximately 12,742 kilometers (7,918 miles), Earth is a diverse and dynamic world.

Mars, the fourth planet from the Sun,is distinguished by its rusty-red appearance and is often referred to as the "Red Planet."With a diameter of around 6,779 kilometers (4,212 miles)

Jupiter, the largest planet in our solar system, commands attention with its immense size and distinctive features. Boasting a colossal diameter of approximately 139,820 kilometers (86,881 miles), Jupiter dwarfs not only Mercury but also all other planets.

Saturn, the sixth planet from the Sun, stands out as a captivating giant in our solar system, characterized by its stunning ring system and unique features. Despite its impressive size, with a diameter of approximately 116,460 kilometers (72,366 miles)

Uranus, the seventh planet from the Sun, presents a unique and enigmatic aspect within our solar system. With a diameter of approximately 51,118 kilometers (31,763 miles).Ice giant's distinctiveness extends to its rotational axis, which is tilted at an extreme angle, causing it to essentially roll on its side as it orbits the Sun.

Neptune is the eighth and farthest known planet from the Sun in our solar system, and it is the fourth-largest planet by diameter. With a diameter of about 49,244 kilometers (30,598 miles).Despite its considerable size, Neptune is smaller than the gas giants Jupiter and Saturn.
